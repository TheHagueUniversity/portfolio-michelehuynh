Rijksmusem App

Research & User Trialling

Deliverable

Design a mobile app that could be used as an educational tool box

Context

Course Project (University, Individual)

Timeline

September 2019 - October 2019 (8 weeks)

Design Brief

UX designers are investigative designers. To better understand our end users as designers, it is integral to understand our users better through research means - to be able to design a product with good usability that can be enjoyed. Research can help us spark inspiration and make founded choices in our design process. For the Research and Design module we learned and conducted user trialling, one of the UX research methods.

Subject

Evaluating usability and experiences of The Rijksmuseum mobile application

This app lets you see all the paintings/exhibitions/favorite museum goer routes within the museum, guides you to exact location/orientation within the museum, allows you to purchase entry tickets, gives you access to the museum shop, and also allows you to create a personal Rijksmuseum account.

Our Aim

Given a massive turn in consumer behavior, mobile apps are now an increasingly popular choice and medium for content and services. With this user trialling, we further examine if the Rijksmuseum app is useful, relevant and valued for its users. To ensure positive and seamless app experience within the app - we will be doing user trialling as we observe and listen to our users engage with the app.

Aim

Finding the usability flaws within the Rijksmuseum app

Usability consists of effectiveness, efficiency and satisfaction. These three are measured by observing use actions, usability problems, and users‚Äô perceptions of performed interactions. Emphasis is placed on the qualitative because only with understanding of interaction difficulties can a design be optimised.

Research Questions

To translate from aim of the project to the base of research we defined a few research questions to lead us from preparations to actual points of interest for our trialling.

1. What do participants in user trialling actually do when using Rijksstudio?

2. How does the app perform on effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction?

3. How does the user flow change with the use of different devices?
